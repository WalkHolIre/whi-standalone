Für wen ist diese Tour?

**Serious walkers and trekkers** who want the full experience rather than the cliff's notes version. If you've completed other long-distance trails (GR20, West Highland Way, etc.), you'll appreciate having time to sink into the rhythm of this one. The pace is manageable but the distance is real.

**People who need to recover properly** from daily hiking. Some walkers discover that six-hour days back-to-back work fine, but others (especially those over 50, or those returning to hiking after a break) find that a shorter daily mileage lets them actually enjoy the experience rather than just grit through it. The 10-day version gives you that breathing room.

**Solo travellers seeking a genuine escape** from the world for ten days. There's something transformative about two weeks away from emails, news cycles, and usual life. Day four or five, something shifts in your nervous system. By day eight, you're a different person.

**People who want to enerstand a place deeply**, not just see it. An 8-day tour is a snapshot. A 10-day tour with a rest day is a proper conversation. You meet locals, you see how the villages change, you enerstand the landscape's rhythms.

**Couples and small groups** looking for quality time outdoors where you're active but not destroyed. This pace lets you talk while walking, stop for photos without stressing about daily targets, and actually have energy to enjoy dinner and conversation each evening.

Reiseverlauf

Day 1

Arrival in Bunclody

expand_more
Welcome to the Wicklow Way. We collect you from Dublin airport and transfer south to Bunclody, a charming border town where the route begins. Time to settle, meet your fellow walkers and guides, and prepare yourself for the next ten days. This is an arrival day—no hiking, just orientation and rest. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B | *Meals:* Breakfast, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 2

Clonegal to Shillelagh — 23 km | 360m ascent | 6.5-7.5 hours

expand_more
Your first full day, and it's a significant one. Starting in the small village of Clonegal, you'll walk through rural Ireland—farmland, country lanes, forest sections—gradually gaining elevation. The ascent is steady but not brutal; you're getting your body adjusted to walking eight to nine hours. By the end, you'll have earned your rest in Shillelagh, a characterful village with real soul. Your legs will feel it, but in that good way. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B in Shillelagh | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 3

Shillelagh to Moyne — 16 km | 370m ascent | 4-5 hours

expand_more
A shorter day to let your body adjust. You're moving into the foothills proper now, moorland appearing, the landscape opening beneath you as you climb. This day is manageable and gives you confidence: "Yes, I can do this." You'll arrive in Moyne with energy still in the tank, which is exactly the point. A good night's sleep and you'll be ready for the big days ahead. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B in Moyne | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 4

Moyne to Glenmalure — 23 km | 795m ascent | 6 hours

expand_more
This is the test. The longest day, the most climbing, the most stunning scenery. You're heading into the heart of Wicklow Mountains through Wicklow Gap, every direction offering extraordinary views. The landscape becomes properly wild. Glenmalure Valley—your destination—feels like you've stepped into another world: remote, dramatic, utterly untamed. This day separates the casual walkers from the committed trekkers. You'll feel like a proper adventurer by the end. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B in Glenmalure | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 5

Glenmalure to Glendalough — 17 km | 521m ascent | 5-6 hours

expand_more
Today you descend from the wild mountains toward one of Ireland's most historically significant places. The path takes you past waterfalls and through forest before opening to the valley containing Glendalough's monastic site. This day is shorter, giving you recovery time before your free day. The energy of approaching this ancient place builds throughout the day. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B in Glendalough | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 6

FREE DAY in Glendalough

expand_more
No hiking, no pressure, no schedule. This is your day. Some people explore the monastic ruins thoroughly—wander among the round towers at different times of day, sit by the lakes, soak in the history. Some sleep in and enjoy a leisurely village breakfast. Some take the optional peak walk to Lough Tay (another 15 km if you want it, or you ignore the mountains entirely and stay valley-boen). Some take a guided tour of local heritage sites. Some simply read a book in a café and let their muscles recover properly. This is the true gift of a 10-day trip: you're not a hiking machine moving from point A to point B. You're a human being experiencing a place. We recommend taking at least one hike on your rest day (you've earned endorphins), but it's your choice entirely. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B in Glendalough | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, lunch & dinner (own arrangement—but we'll recommend excellent local spots)
Day 7

Glendalough to Lough Tay — 20 km | 651m ascent | 5-6 hours

expand_more
Back on the trail with renewed legs and spirit. Today you climb to the most photographed lake in Ireland: Lough Tay, sitting impossibly blue (or dark, depending on light) in its mountain cup. The terrain here is genuinely high and exposed—you're on proper mountain ridges with 360-degree views. On clear days you can see across to Dublin Bay and the coast beyond. This day is exhilarating after your rest. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B near Lough Tay | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 8

Lough Tay to Enniskerry — 17 km | 410m ascent | 4.5-5.5 hours

expand_more
The mountains begin their transition to foothills. You're descending from high alpine country toward the gentler landscape surroening Dublin. Forests, managed woodlands, and the first signs of suburban life appearing. Enniskerry is an attractive village with character—good restaurants, friendly pubs, and the sense that you're approaching the end of your journey. A slightly easier day lets your legs prepare for the final push. *Accommodation:* En-suite B&B in Enniskerry | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, dinner (own arrangement)
Day 9

Enniskerry to Marlay Park — 22 km | 605m ascent | 6-7 hours

expand_more
The final day. Today you complete the entire Wicklow Way, finishing at Marlay Park on Dublin's southern edge. The last serious climbing gives you a triumphant finish—you're not walking downhill into the city, but over ridges and through final mountain sections before Dublin gradually surroens you. By the time you reach Marlay Park, you've properly earned your completion. Tired, exhilarated, transformed. The sense of achievement is enormous. *Accommodation:* Dublin area | *Meals:* Full Irish breakfast, packed lunch, celebration dinner (own arrangement)
Day 10

Departure from Dublin

expand_more
Transfer to airport or city centre. Ten days in, you're a different person. Your fitness is entirely different. Your mind is clearer. Your legs are stronger. And you've got stories—real conversations with locals, moments where you were testing yourself, mountains you've touched, valleys you've properly inhabited.

Accommodatie

Your home each night is a carefully selected **en-suite bed & breakfast**, the kind of genuine Irish guesthouse where the owners enerstand what walkers need and appreciate having people who actually care about their accommodation's quality. Real beds, hot showers, and that legendary Irish breakfast each morning—the kind with proper black pudding, sausages, eggs, and toast that gets you ready for hours of mountain.

The accommodation is strategically positioned so your daily walks remain manageable despite the longer total distance, and our **daily luggage transfer service** means you carry only a light daypack (5-8 kg). Your main pack—the heavy stuff—travels ahead by van to the next B&B, which is genuinely the only way to walk comfortably for ten consecutive days.

Here's something important: these aren't corporate chain hotels or boutique Instagraan places. They're homes, run by local people who've spent decades welcoming walkers. You'll get local knowledge, genuine warmth, and the kind of welcome that makes Ireland feel special.

Veelgestelde Vragen

How long is the Wicklow Way? expand_more
The full Wicklow Way is 127 kilometres. We offer it in versions of 5, 7, 8, and 10 days to suit different fitness levels and time available.
Wat is inbegrepen in een zelfbegeleide wandelvakantie? expand_more
Our self-guided tours include: carefully selected family-run B&B accommodation, comfortable en-suite bedrooms (where available), a full Irish breakfast each morning, complimentary luggage transfers between accommodations, detailed maps and route notes, local advice on places to visit and eat, full pre-departure information, a waterproof map case, and 24/7 local emergency contact support. Extra nights and local transport information can be arranged on request.
Wat is NIET inbegrepen in de tourprijs? expand_more
The tour price does not include flights, travel and baggage insurance, lunches, or evening meals.
Is een GPS-app of digitale navigatie inbegrepen? expand_more
Yes. Our tours include a digital route guide with GPS navigation, offline maps, and local insights via our mobile app, so you can navigate confidently even without mobile signal.
Is 24/7-noodondersteuning echt beschikbaar? expand_more
Yes. Our teaan provides 24/7 local emergency contact support throughout your tour. If you encounter any difficulties on the trail or with your accommodation, you can reach us at any time.
Zijn avondmaaltijden inbegrepen? expand_more
Evening meals are not included in the standard tour price. Your B&B hosts can usually recommend nearby restaurants, and many guesthouses can arrange an evening meal on request.
Zijn lunches inbegrepen? expand_more
Lunches are not included. However, you will typically have the opportunity to purchase picnic lunches, and we provide local advice on where to eat along the route.
Wat is het verschil tussen een zelfbegeleide en een geleide wandeltour? expand_more
On a self-guided tour, you walk independently using our detailed maps, route notes, and GPS app, with your luggage transferred each day and 24/7 support available. A guided tour provides a local expert guide who walks with you, shares local knowledge, stories and history, and ensures you stay on track and safe. Guided tours also include built-in companionship with fellow walkers.
