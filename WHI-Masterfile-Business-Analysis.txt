PART 2: THE ROUTES (PRODUCT CATALOGUE)
================================================================================

All tours are self-guided. Luggage transfer is standard on all routes.
All routes are point-to-point or loop, bookable in multiple durations.

1. DINGLE WAY
   - Status: Most popular route — lead product
   - Region: Dingle Peninsula, Co. Kerry (Wild Atlantic Way)
   - Character: Coastal, dramatic, iconic Irish landscape
   - Key selling points: Wild Atlantic coastline, ancient heritage sites,
     remote beauty, one of Ireland's most iconic walks
   - Market appeal: Appeals strongly to EN, DE, NL markets

2. KERRY WAY
   - Status: Second most popular. Well-established.
   - Region: Ring of Kerry / Iveragh Peninsula, Co. Kerry
   - Character: Mountainous, varied terrain, famous scenery
   - Key selling points: Macgillycuddy's Reeks, Killarney National Park,
     mix of wild upland and coastal scenery

3. WICKLOW WAY
   - Status: Very popular — strong seller, particularly with Dutch/European market
   - Region: Wicklow Mountains, Co. Wicklow (closest mountains to Dublin)
   - Character: Upland moorland, forest, valleys, accessible
   - Key selling points: Easy access from Dublin, varied terrain, 6- and 7-day
     options, good for first-time multi-day walkers
   - Custom itineraries: Cliff creates bespoke 6-day/7-day versions

4. BARROW WAY
   - Region: River Barrow towpath, Leinster
   - Character: Flat, gentle, river towpath. Accessible for all fitness levels.
   - Key selling points: Easy walking, canal/river scenery, heritage towns,
     great for less-experienced or older walkers

5. BURREN WAY
   - Region: The Burren, Co. Clare
   - Character: Unique limestone karst landscape, wildflowers, ancient sites
   - Key selling points: Completely unique Irish landscape, UNESCO area nearby,
     Cliffs of Moher proximity

6. CAUSEWAY COAST WAY
   - Region: North Antrim Coast, Northern Ireland
   - Character: Dramatic coastal cliffs, famous landmarks
   - Key selling points: Giant's Causeway UNESCO site, Carrick-a-Rede rope bridge,
     stunning cliff scenery, Northern Ireland cross-border appeal

7. COOLEY & MOURNES
   - Region: Co. Louth / Co. Down
   - Character: Quieter, less-toured mountain landscapes
   - Key selling points: Off-the-beaten-track, near Dundalk (home territory),
     Mourne Mountains scenery, less crowded than Kerry routes

TAILOR-MADE TOURS:
  - Core differentiator vs. booking platforms / aggregators
  - Any route, any duration, any group size
  - Custom start dates
  - Mixed ability accommodation
  - This is a key section of the website and should be prominently featured

================================================================================

BRAND POSITIONING:
"The personal, family-run alternative to impersonal booking platforms."
WHI occupies the premium-personal segment: not the cheapest, not the most
corporate — the most human. Cliff and Louise ARE the brand.

BRAND PERSONALITY:
  - Warm and personal (not corporate)
  - Knowledgeable local insiders (not generic travel agents)
  - Adventurous but reassuring (not extreme, not bland)
  - Authentic Irish (not theme-park Irish)
  - Reliable and detail-oriented (not casual or vague)

BRAND VOICE (summary — full guide in seo-copywriter/references/brand-voice.md):
  - Speak like a knowledgeable Irish friend, not a travel brochure
  - Use "you" and "your" constantly — the walker is the hero
  - Reference personal experiences on the trails
  - Celebrate the achievement of walking long distances
  - CTAs are friendly invitations, never hard sells

COLOUR PALETTE (CONFIRMED — DO NOT DEVIATE):
  Primary orange:     #F17E00  (warm, energetic, Irish)
  Dark purple:        #210747  (depth, premium, contrast)
  Bright purple:      #3F0F87  (accent, digital vibrancy)
  Mauve/lilac:        #B58DB6  (soft accent, approachable)

  EXPLICITLY EXCLUDED: Shamrock green, royal blue, gold
  (These are Irish clichés — WHI is deliberately different)

TYPOGRAPHY DIRECTION:
  - Clean, readable, modern
  - Strong hierarchy: H1 large and bold, body text generous size
  - No decorative "Paddy" fonts — no shamrocks, Celtic knotwork in typography
  - Professional but warm

PHOTOGRAPHY DIRECTION:
  - Real walkers on real trails (not stock photo models)
  - Landscape-first: show the scenery they are walking through
  - Weather variety: misty, sunny, dramatic skies — Ireland's weather is part of the charm
  - No clichéd "green Ireland" photo overuse
  - Show B&Bs and accommodation — cosy, personal, real

PART 7: CONVERSION STRATEGY
================================================================================

THE FUNNEL:
  AWARENESS → Google search / social media
  CONSIDERATION → Tour pages, blog, About page
  DECISION → Contact form / email enquiry
  BOOKING → Personal email/phone exchange with Cliff or Louise
  ADVOCACY → Reviews, referrals, repeat bookings

KEY CONVERSION PRINCIPLES:

1. TRUST FIRST
   Visitors are planning a significant trip (1–2 weeks, substantial spend).
   They need to trust WHI before anything else. Trust signals:
   - Real photos of Cliff and Louise on About page
   - Customer testimonials on every key page
   - "Established 2012" prominently featured
   - Response time guarantee on contact page
   - TripAdvisor / Google reviews widget if possible

2. VALUE IS CLEAR BEFORE PRICE
   Show what's included in detail BEFORE showing the price.
   Luggage transfer is a surprise delight — many visitors don't know this exists.
   Make the value proposition undeniable before the cost lands.

3. REDUCE FRICTION TO ENQUIRY
   Don't force visitors to book without talking first.
   The CTA is "get in touch" — not "book now and pay."
   Many of WHI's best customers want to discuss their trip first.
   Make the enquiry form very simple (5 fields max).

4. TESTIMONIALS ARE CRITICAL
   Real testimonials from real walkers (by name, nationality, route walked)
   are the single highest-converting trust element on the site.
   Aim for: at least 3 testimonials per route page, 6–10 on homepage.

5. URGENCY WITHOUT PRESSURE
   Mention the season runs April–October.
   Mention popular dates book up in advance.
   Never use countdown timers or "only 2 spots left!" fake urgency.

6. MOBILE-FIRST
   Most initial research happens on mobile (scrolling in bed, on commute).
   The site must be perfectly usable on a phone.
   CTA buttons must be thumb-friendly and above the fold on mobile.

PART 8: PRICING & COMMERCIAL FRAMEWORK
================================================================================

NOTE: Exact prices are not recorded here. This section covers structure.

PRICING MODEL:
  - Price per person, based on double occupancy
  - Solo supplement applied for single travellers
  - Duration variants (shorter and longer versions of same route)
  - "Price from €X" on marketing materials — full quote on enquiry

PRICE POSITIONING:
  - Mid-to-premium range
  - Never compete on price with cheap booking platforms
  - Value justification: personal service, handpicked accommodation,
    luggage transfer, 24/7 support, tailor-made options
  - Emphasise what's INCLUDED — not just the headline number

WHAT'S INCLUDED (standard):
  - All accommodation (B&B, en suite where possible)
  - Luggage transfer daily (bag delivered to next accommodation)
  - Detailed route maps and notes
  - Emergency support phone line
  - Pre-trip planning assistance

WHAT'S NOT INCLUDED (standard):
  - Flights / transport to Ireland
  - Meals (breakfast typically included at B&B, evening meals self-arranged)
  - Travel insurance (always recommend)
  - Personal kit / equipment
