Historical Sites To Visit On Your Irish Hiking Journey 

W

Walking Holiday Ireland

August 26, 2023

Finding remarkable historical sites to visit during an Irish hiking journey can be a bit overwhelming. Ireland, famed for its stunning landscapes, is also rich in history with ancient ruins and castles dotting the countryside.

This article will guide you through some of Ireland's most significant landmarks that hold captivating narratives from diverse eras. Ready for a time-travelling adventure on foot? Let’s start exploring!

Key Takeaways

  • Giant's Causeway is a stunning natural wonder with 40,000 basalt columns in Northern Ireland.
  • The Derry Walls in Northern Ireland offer a step back in time to experience the history and culture of Derry.
  • Clonmacnoise is an ancient monastery with ruins, round towers, and High Crosses dating back to Early Christian times.
  • The Rock of Cashel boasts old towers and ruins from ancient times, making it one of Ireland's most impressive historic sights.
  • The Hill of Tara holds great historical value as a burial site and offers breathtaking views of Boyne Valley.
  • Other notable historical sites include Leap Castle, Dunbrody Famine Ship, Céide Fields, Glendalough, and GPO in Dublin.

Must-Visit Historical Sites in Ireland

Visit the stunning Giant's Causeway, explore the historic Derry Walls, discover the ancient site of Clonmacnoise, marvel at the Rock of Cashel, and immerse yourself in the history of Ireland at the Hill of Tara.

Giant’s Causeway

Giant's Causeway is a must-see in Northern Ireland. It has 40,000 basalt columns that lock together. An old volcano made these columns many years ago. They sit at the edge of steep cliffs by the sea coast in Northern Ireland.

You can see them drop into the deep blue sea from far off hills. People from all over come to see this wonder of nature!

The Derry Walls

The Derry Walls stand big and strong in Northern Ireland. They were built in the early 17th century to keep the city safe. These walls are very old but they are still whole. No other city in Ireland has a full set of walls like Derry.

Walking on these walls is like walking back in time. You can feel and see the history, culture, and heritage of Derry on each step you take. These great walls tell rich stories of times good and bad from long ago.

Their charm draws a lot of people each year who come to learn about Irish history.

Clonmacnoise

Clonmacnoise is a must-visit historical site in Ireland. This famous monastery sits in County Offaly. It came to life in 544 and quickly became one of the top Early Christian monastic sites.

The place was a hot spot for its strong scholarship over the years, with many European scholars drawn towards it between the 9th and 11th centuries. A large number of ruins make up Clonmacnoise today, including two round towers, three high crosses, nine churches, and over 700 Early Christian graveslabs! You can even catch sight of some original High Crosses here like the well-known Cross of Scriptures.

Rock of Cashel

The Rock of Cashel is a must-visit spot in Ireland. It sits high on a large slab of limestone, looking out over the Golden Vale. This site boasts old towers and ruins from ancient times; King Aengus once called it home.

Now, tourists flock here to see its round tower, Romanesque church, and Gothic cathedral built during the 12th and 13th centuries. Many say The Rock of Cashel is one of Ireland's most stunning historic sights to see.

Hill of Tara

The Hill of Tara is a true gem of Ireland's past. For more than 5000 years, it was used for burying the dead and as a place to pray. It is an old site in Europe that holds great value.

Many Irish kings wanted this spot due to its power. Besides its deep roots in history, it also gives stunning views of Boyne Valley.

Other Notable Historical Sites

Other notable historical sites in Ireland include Leap Castle, the Dunbrody Famine Ship, the Céide Fields, Glendalough, and the General Post Office (GPO).

https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=N9ZZ6zNiSro

Leap Castle

Leap Castle is a gothic wonder in County Offaly, Ireland. It has been standing tall for over 700 years. This castle tells tales of intense history, love affairs and lives lost. It also boasts of an eerie past with its hidden dungeon named 'oubliette,' where prisoners were left to die.

The castle's dark history makes it one of the most haunted locations in the world. People talk about ghost sightings and strange happenings here.

Dunbrody Famine Ship

The Dunbrody Famine Ship is a reproduction of an 1840s emigrant ship that offers visitors the chance to experience what it was like for Irish emigrants during the famine period. Located in the town of New Ross in the South-East of Ireland, this historical site allows you to step aboard and see firsthand the conditions that these brave souls endured on their journey.

In addition to exploring the ship, visitors can also check out other nearby attractions like the Hook Lighthouse and Tintern Abbey. The Dunbrody Famine Ship provides guided tours, exhibits, and educational programs for a truly immersive experience.

Céide Fields

Céide Fields is an ancient historical site in County Mayo, Ireland. It is known for having one of the oldest stone-walled field systems in the world. This fascinating archaeological site dates back 6,000 years to around 4,000 BCE during the Stone Age.

Located on the north County Mayo coast, about 4.3 miles northwest of Ballycastle, Céide Fields offers a unique glimpse into prehistoric farming practices and life from thousands of years ago.

The extensive field systems at Céide Fields provide valuable insights into how people cultivated and managed land in ancient times. Exploring this ancient site allows visitors to connect with our distant past and appreciate the ingenuity of early farmers.

Glendalough

Glendalough is a famous pilgrimage site and historical attraction in Ireland. It is an ancient location that dates back to the medieval era. The monastic settlement in Glendalough was founded by St.

Kevin, who was known for his miracles and impact on the area. People visit Glendalough to explore its Early Medieval period ruins and to learn about the history of this beloved site.

General Post Office (GPO)

The General Post Office (GPO) in Dublin is a must-visit historical site with great significance. It was the headquarters of the Irish Post Office and played a key role in the 1916 Easter Rising, an important event in Irish history.

Completed in 1818, this iconic symbol of Dublin served as the base for rebel operations during the Rising. Today, it houses the GPO Museum, where you can experience an immersive journey through Irish history and learn about the events of the Easter Rising and its impact on modern Ireland.

Historic National Parks in Ireland

Explore the stunning landscapes and rich history of Ireland's historic national parks, such as The Burren, Skellig Michael, Carrowmore, Drombeg Stone Circle, and Ardmore Cathedral and Round Tower.

The Burren

The Burren National Park in County Clare, Ireland is a must-visit historic national park. Covering an area of 1,800 hectares, it is internationally famous for its unique moon-like landscape.

The Burren is part of the UNESCO Geopark and is known for its ancient monuments and historical sites. Exploring this park allows you to immerse yourself in the rich history of Ireland while enjoying the stunning Irish countryside.

Skellig Michael

Skellig Michael, also known as Star Wars Island, is a must-visit historical site off the western coast of Ireland in County Kerry. This island gained fame for its appearance in the Star Wars film series.

It is home to a well-preserved early Christian monastic settlement that has been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Exploring Skellig Michael allows travelers to witness remarkable ruins and marvel at the 750-feet tall twin-pinnacled crag called Great Skellig.

To fully experience the historic sites on this island, visitors can take a Landing Tour.

Carrowmore

Carrowmore is a historic national park in County Sligo, Ireland. It is famous for being the oldest and most densely populated burial ground from the Neolithic period in all of Ireland.

The site, which dates back between 5700 and 5000 years ago, features an impressive collection of ancient tombs and monuments. You can explore stone circles, dolmens, prehistoric remains, and burial mounds that belonged to ancient civilizations.

Carrowmore is considered one of the most important megalithic sites in Europe and offers a fascinating glimpse into Ireland's rich history.

Drombeg Stone Circle

Drombeg Stone Circle is a famous ancient site located in County Cork, Ireland. It is an archaeological monument that dates back to the Bronze Age, estimated to be around 2,000 to 1,500 BC.

The stone circle consists of seventeen closely spaced pillars and is considered one of the most significant prehistoric stone circles in Ireland. Drombeg Stone Circle holds great historical and cultural importance as part of Ireland's rich ancient heritage.

Ardmore Cathedral and Round Tower

Ardmore Cathedral and Round Tower are important historical sites in Ireland. In the 5th century, St. Declan founded a monastery in Ardmore. The Round Tower, built in the 12th century, is still well-preserved today.

It was part of Ireland's Golden Age of Saints and Scholars. Besides the tower, there is also a church and grave area associated with it. If you visit Ardmore, don't miss the stunning views along the Ardmore Cliff Walk and explore the remains of St.

Declan's Church.

Conclusion

Discover the fascinating history of Ireland as you embark on an unforgettable hiking journey. From ancient ruins to medieval fortresses, there are countless historical sites waiting to be explored.

Immerse yourself in Ireland's rich heritage and uncover the secrets of its past while enjoying breathtaking natural landscapes. So lace up your hiking boots and get ready for a truly captivating adventure through Ireland's historic landmarks!

FAQs

1. Are the historical sites on an Irish hiking journey easily accessible?

Most of the historical sites on an Irish hiking journey are easily accessible, although some may require hiking or walking to reach.

2. Can I visit these historical sites for free?

Many of the historical sites on an Irish hiking journey offer free admission, while others may have a small entrance fee.

3. How long should I allocate to visit each historical site?

The time needed to visit each historical site can vary, but generally, allocating 1-2 hours per site allows enough time to explore and learn about its history.

4. Can I take photographs at the historical sites?

Yes, photography is usually allowed at the historical sites on an Irish hiking journey unless otherwise stated. However, it's respectful to check for any specific restrictions or guidelines.

5. Do I need a guide to visit these historical sites?

While having a guide can provide deeper insights into the history and significance of each site, visiting without a guide is still possible as most locations have informational plaques or boards that provide details about their importance.
