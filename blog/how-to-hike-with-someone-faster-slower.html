Hiking with Faster & Slower Partners: Making Group Hiking Work

One of the most common hiking challenges doesn't involve weather, terrain, or fitness—it's managing groups where members hike at different paces. A fast hiker with a slow hiker, a 25-year-old with a 60-year-old, an experienced mountaineer with a first-timer—pace differences are inevitable when groups hike together. But they're entirely manageable with clear communication, smart strategies, and mutual respect.
