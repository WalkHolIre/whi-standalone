Best Challenging Hiking Routes in Ireland

Discover the thrilling allure of challenging hiking routes in Ireland, from the Great Sugar Loaf in County Wicklow to Carrauntoohill in County Kerry.

The Great Sugar Loaf, County Wicklow

The Great Sugar Loaf in County Wicklow is a must-see! This hike takes you up rugged quartzite slopes. It's not easy, but it's worth it. From the top of Sugarloaf Mountain, you get an amazing view.

You can see all around from this high point. The trail is about 3.3 miles long and usually takes more than two hours to finish. Many love this challenging walk in the Wicklow Mountains because it offers great sights and a good test for hikers too.

Carrauntoohill, County Kerry

Carrauntoohill is the highest mountain in Ireland and lies in County Kerry. Climbing this peak is a challenge, but it offers great views. The most liked trail here is the Devil's Ladder.

It takes you up 20 km of rough paths to reach the top. This makes Carrauntoohill a top spot for challenging hiking in Ireland.

Yet, only skilled hikers should dare to take on Carrauntoohill. You will want to be safe while enjoying your hike and exploring this tall Irish mountain range. Its height and tough trails place it among the best challenging hiking routes in Ireland.

Mweelrea, County Mayo

Mweelrea in County Mayo is not an easy walk. It has a height of 814m, making it the tallest peak in the area. The mountain's rough terrain makes for a tough hike, but also an exciting one.

If you plan to go here, bring your own map! There are no signs or paths to help guide you up the route. Despite these challenges, many climbers love Mweelrea and even take part in the Mweelrea Mountains Challenge to reach every top point.

Mt Errigal, County Donegal

Mt Errigal in County Donegal is a tall peak. It stands at a height of 751m. Hiking up this mountain is not easy. You need to make a good try and know how to get around mountains. But now, there is a gravel path that goes all the way up, which makes the climb easier.

The hike can be tough but it's worth it as you go high up on this route. It takes about 2 hours and 44 minutes for most people to finish the hike.

Lugnaquilla, County Wicklow

Lugnaquilla is a high mountain in County Wicklow, Ireland. It sits tall in the middle of the Wicklow Mountains and it's the tallest one around, except for those in Kerry County. Many people start their hike from Baravore carpark that is at the end of Glenmalure Valley.

The trail going up Lugnaquilla can be tough as there are no marked routes on it. Hikers should prepare for a good workout because this walk can range from 10 to 16 kilometers long! This means you could be hiking for about 5 to 8 hours but trust us, the view will be worth every step.

Scenic Hiking Routes in Ireland

Some of the most picturesque hiking routes in Ireland include the Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop in County Laois, the Ballinastoe to Djouce trail in County Wicklow, and the Croaghan Cliffs in County Mayo.

These routes offer stunning views of waterfalls, forests, and cliffs that will take your breath away.

Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop, County Laois

Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop is your gateway to an outdoor adventure. It lies in County Laois, in the scenic Slieve Bloom region of Ireland. You start and end at Glenbarrow trailhead for this hike.

This trail takes you along the River Barrow offering stunning riverbank views. The main attraction of this loop is the Glenbarrow Waterfall. It's a sight that makes this walk worth every step!

Ballinastoe to Djouce, County Wicklow

Ballinastoe to Djouce is a must-see trail in County Wicklow, Ireland. This long path is 17.4 km and forms a loop that starts and ends at the same spot. As you walk, you'll pass through Ballinastoe Woods, climb up White Hill and reach Djouce Mountain's peak.

This route forms part of a much bigger national hiking path that covers 128 km from Rathfarnham to Clonegal! Usually, people take about 6 hours to finish this hard but beautiful hike near Calary.

Croaghan Cliffs, County Mayo

Croaghan Cliffs in County Mayo are the highest sea cliffs in Ireland, offering stunning and scenic views. These cliffs can only be seen by hiking around or to them, making it an adventurous experience for visitors.

There is a challenging 4.9-mile out-and-back trail near Keel that takes around 4 hours and 26 minutes to complete. Hikers can expect breathtaking scenery and panoramic vistas along the way.

Croaghan Cliffs provide an opportunity for outdoor enthusiasts to enjoy the beauty of nature while tackling one of the highest points in Ireland's landscape.

Lough Ouler Loop, County Wicklow

The Lough Ouler Loop is a challenging hiking trail located in County Wicklow, Ireland. This scenic route offers breathtaking views of the surrounding area, including the beautiful Wicklow Mountains.

However, be prepared for some tough terrain as there are many boggy sections along the way and parts of the trail can be quite muddy. One of the highlights of this hike is reaching Lough Ouler, a heart-shaped lake nestled in the mountains.

It's worth noting that access to this stunning lake is only possible on foot via various trails. So if you're up for a challenge and want to experience spectacular scenery, don't miss out on exploring the Lough Ouler Loop in County Wicklow.

Bray to Greystones Cliff Walk, County Wicklow

The Bray to Greystones Cliff Walk in County Wicklow is a scenic hiking route that stretches for 7 kilometers along the Irish coastline. This walk offers breathtaking views of the ocean, green fields, and wildlife.

It is known for its stunning scenery, with dramatic cliffs and lush hillsides. Along the way, you can spot native wildlife such as Common Lizards basking in the sun. Whether you enjoy nature walks or want to take in the beauty of Ireland's landscapes, this coastal trail is a must-visit.

You'll be captivated by the picturesque sights and have plenty of opportunities for outdoor adventure in County Wicklow.

Self-Guided Walking Tours in Ireland - The Wicklow Way

The Wicklow Way is a self-guided walking tour in the east of Ireland. It is actually Ireland's oldest way-marked trail, and it covers over 127 kilometers from Dublin to Carlow. This tour offers challenging yet scenic hiking routes through various landscapes in the Wicklow region.

Along the way, travelers will be able to admire breathtaking views and explore highlights of the area. The accommodations chosen for this tour are known for their excellent location, service, and comfort.

This affordable 5-day walking holiday allows you to experience the beauty of Ireland at your own pace while enjoying the benefits of a well-planned itinerary.

FAQs

1. Are the hiking routes in Ireland challenging?

Yes, the hiking routes in Ireland are known for their challenging nature, with steep ascents, rugged terrains, and varying levels of difficulty.

2. Which hiking route in Ireland offers scenic views?

The Wicklow Way is a popular hiking route in Ireland that offers stunning scenic views of mountains, lakes, forests, and countryside.

3. How long does it take to complete a typical hike in Ireland?

The duration of a hike in Ireland varies depending on the chosen trail and individual pace. It can range from a few hours to multiple days for longer routes like the Kerry Way or the Dingle Peninsula Loop.

4. Do I need special gear or equipment for hiking in Ireland?

It is recommended to have appropriate gear such as sturdy footwear with good traction, waterproof clothing, sun protection, navigation tools like maps or GPS devices, and sufficient food and water for longer hikes.

5. Can beginners attempt these challenging hiking routes in Ireland?

While some hiking routes may be more suitable for experienced hikers due to their difficulty level, there are also trails available that cater to beginners or those with less experience. It's important to choose a route that matches your skill level and prepare accordingly.
