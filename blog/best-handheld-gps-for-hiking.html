What is GPS and Why Do You Need One for Hiking and Backpacking?

Understanding GPS Technology

GPS, which stands for Global Positioning System, is a satellite navigation technology that allows users to determine their approximate location. Hiking GPS devices are specially designed for outdoor activities and offer various features beneficial to hikers and backpackers. Unlike standard GPS models, these rugged devices are built to withstand the elements.

How Does GPS Work?

GPS operates via a network of satellites orbiting Earth. At its core are Global Navigation Satellite Systems (GNSS), including the American GPS and other systems like Russian GLONASS. Your device, whether a tracker or a more advanced satellite communicator and GPS, connects to these satellites to determine your precise location.

  • Global Navigation Satellite: This term refers to any satellite involved in the navigation or positioning system. Each satellite in these systems continuously transmits data about its location and the current time to Earth.
  • GPS Receiver: The GPS receiver in your handheld unit or smartphone captures these transmissions. By connecting with at least four satellites, the receiver can accurately triangulate your position.
  • GPS and GLONASS: Many modern GPS devices utilize American and Russian GLONASS satellites to improve accuracy, selecting the best satellite signals from among them for the most precise positioning possible.

Combining these elements allows for precise backcountry navigation, whether hiking, backpacking, or engaging in other outdoor activities.

Do You Really Need a GPS Device?

While smartphones may offer GPS capabilities, they're not the best option for serious hiking or backpacking. Many of the best handheld GPS devices are designed to work for extended periods, especially compared to smartphones. Additionally, they often feature better battery life, detailed maps, and more robust navigation features, making them more suited for backcountry navigation and discovering the best hiking locations in Ireland.

How to Choose the Best Handheld GPS for Hiking?

Consider the Battery Life

Battery life is crucial when you're out in the wilderness. The best handheld GPS devices of 2024 offer varying battery life, often ranging from 16 to 50 hours in standard GPS mode. When choosing a handheld GPS unit for your hiking or backpacking trips, it's vital to consider your usage and how often you'll be able to recharge.

GPS Features You Can't Ignore

When shopping for a GPS device, look beyond the basic functionality. Many handheld GPS devices of 2024 also offer features like an altimeter, barometer, and even satellite communicator. These additional features can significantly enhance your hiking experience.

Garmin vs Other Brands: A Comparison

Garmin GPS Devices

Garmin is a name synonymous with GPS technology. They offer a wide range of models, including the Garmin Montana 700i GPS device, known for its long battery life, excellent map functionality, and additional features like satellite communication.

Magellan and TomTom

While Garmin dominates the GPS market, other brands like Magellan and TomTom shouldn't be overlooked. Magellan offers GPS units with similar features but at a different price point, while TomTom is known for its user-friendly interface.

Is Offline Navigation an Essential Feature?

The Importance of Offline Maps

Not all areas you'll hike or backpack in will have a reliable data connection. Therefore, offline navigation is crucial. Most good-quality GPS units will allow you to download and use maps offline, which can be a lifesaver in remote areas.

Gaia GPS App as an Alternative

The Gaia GPS app can serve as a budget-friendly alternative. It allows offline map downloads but requires you to use your phone, which might not be as durable or have as long battery life as a dedicated GPS device.

GPS Watches: Are They A Good Alternative?

Advantages and Limitations

GPS watches offer a more compact way to track your hike or backpacking route. However, their smaller screen size and limited battery life compared to the best handheld GPS units might make them less suitable for serious backpacking trips.

Best GPS Watches in 2024

If you opt for a GPS watch, some of the best models in 2024 include brands like Garmin. These watches offer long battery life and various hiking-related features, making them suitable for shorter trips.

To Buy or Not to Buy: Do You Really Need a New GPS?

When Not to Buy a GPS

If you’re not a frequent hiker or if you mainly stick to well-trodden paths, you may find that a basic GPS app on your smartphone suffices for your needs. Some argue that you don't necessarily need to buy a GPS device for infrequent, short trips.

When to Invest in a GPS Device

Investing in the best handheld GPS device is advisable if you plan a multi-day hike, go off the beaten path, or hike regularly as a hobby. It provides reliability and features that smartphone GPS can't match.

GPS Tips and Tricks

Offering additional advice on how to get the most out of the device. To get the most out of your handheld GPS device, try using it on different hikes and in different weather conditions. Experiment with features and settings to find what works best for you.

  • Preload Maps: Never head out without preloaded maps on your handheld GPS device.
  • Software Updates: Keep your GPS device up-to-date.
  • Calibrate the GPS Compass: For accurate navigation.
  • Set Waypoints: Plan your route effectively.
  • Use 'Trackback': A lifesaver when you get lost.
  • Monitor Battery Life: Always carry a backup.
  • Watch Signal Strength: Be mindful in tricky terrains.
  • Customize Display: Make it personal to your trek.
  • Have a GPS App Backup: Two is better than one.
  • Test Before Trip: Better safe than sorry.

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some common questions related to the best handheld GPS devices:

Do I need a hiking GPS device if I have a smartphone? 

While smartphones can be useful for navigation, they are less reliable than dedicated GPS devices. Smartphones rely on a cellular network, which may not be available in remote areas. They also have shorter battery life and are less durable than hiking GPS

Can I use my hiking GPS device for geocaching?

Many hiking GPS devices have geocaching features that allow you to search for and find geocaches.

How do I know if my hiking GPS device is accurate?

You can test the accuracy of your device by comparing it to a known point or landmark. You can also use multiple devices to compare their readings.

Conclusion

A hiking GPS device is an essential tool for any hiker. It provides valuable information about your location and helps you navigate through unfamiliar terrain. When choosing a hiking GPS device, consider your needs, budget, and brand reputation. The top 10 best hiking GPS devices for 2024 offer a range of features to meet any hiker's needs. Choose a device that is durable, has a long battery life, is accurate, has good maps and navigation features, is user-friendly, and has connectivity options.

When choosing the best handheld GPS for hiking, accuracy should be a top priority. Look for devices that utilize advanced GPS technology and have a strong reputation for providing accurate location data. Having a dependable and accurate hiking GPS can be a lifesaver in unfamiliar terrain.
