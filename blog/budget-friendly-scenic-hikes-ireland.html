Budget-Friendly Scenic Hikes In Ireland

Photo: Walking Holiday Ireland

Finding budget-friendly yet absolutely mesmerizing hikes in Ireland can feel like a wild goose chase. Yet, the Emerald Isle boasts of incredible trails known for their affordability and breathtaking views.

Key Takeaways

  • Ireland offers a variety of budget - friendly scenic hikes that are perfect for nature lovers.
  • Some top - rated affordable hikes in Ireland include The Great Sugar Loaf, Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop, Bray to Greystones Cliff Walk, Croagh Patrick, and Ballinastoe to Djouce.
  • Tips for hiking in Ireland on a budget include buying Experience Ireland for discounted activities, exploring free or low-cost hikes, using public transportation, packing your own food and water, and considering camping instead of staying in accommodations.
  • Recommended easy walks and hikes with epic views include Benbulben Forest Walk in Sligo, Downpatrick Head in Mayo, Keshcorran Caves in Sligo, the Slieve League Viewing Platform in Donegal, and Diamond Hill - Lower Diamond Trail in Connemara.

Top Rated Scenic Hikes in Ireland

Ireland has some top-rated scenic hikes. One of these is the Great Sugar Loaf in County Wicklow. It's an easy walk that stretches over 2.5km (1.5 miles). Families love it! Another one is the Coomloughra Horseshoe in County Kerry.

This hike might ask a bit more from you, but the views of Coomloughra Lough and the ridgeline are worth it!

Ballycotton Cliff Walk in Cork and Divis and Black Mountain ridge trail in Belfast make up two more of Ireland's best hikes. People say they offer some of the most beautiful sights! Then there is also The Cuilcagh Legnabrocky Trail or as many call it, "Stairway to Heaven," in County Fermanagh.

They say this hike is one of Ireland's best.

Finally, if you like long walks, try The Kerry Way! This path runs for a whole 214-km (133-mile) stretch over 12 days through awesome sights all around Ireland.

Top Budget-Friendly Scenic Hikes in Ireland

Experience the breathtaking beauty of Ireland on a budget with these top-rated scenic hikes that won't break the bank.

The Great Sugar Loaf, County Wicklow

The Great Sugar Loaf sits in County Wicklow, Ireland. It stands tall and can be seen from Dublin as you drive south into Wicklow. This mountain marks the skyline with its high peak.

You can hike up The Great Sugar Loaf for a small cost. All hikers, no matter their skill level, will find it easy to go up this mountain. Once at the top, you'll see great views of Dublin Bay and other mountains nearby.

Many suggest taking the Sugarloaf Loop Trail to reach the top of this beautiful spot.

Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop, County Laois

Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop in County Laois is a top budget-friendly hike. This trail offers scenic views of the peaceful River Barrow. You walk through a pine forest that leads uphill.

The main sight is the stunning Glenbarrow waterfall, which takes your breath away. Some parts of the path are muddy and uneven, so hiking boots are needed.

Bray to Greystones Cliff Walk, County Wicklow

The Bray to Greystones Cliff Walk is a great hike in County Wicklow. This 7km trail goes along the Irish coastline and green hillsides. You will see stunning views all along the way.

It is one of Wicklow's top walks, and many families from Dublin love it too! The walk starts and ends at train stations in Bray and Greystones. Along this trail, you get to enjoy looking at the ocean and wide open fields.

Croagh Patrick, County Mayo

Croagh Patrick is a tall mountain in County Mayo. It stands very high, at 764m. Many people see it as a holy place in Ireland. There is an old religious site there from the 11th century.

The path up the mountain is hard to walk on because it's steep and full of small rocks. But your work pays off with stunning views! You can see Clew Bay, the edge of the Atlantic Ocean, and small islands called drumlin islands from up top.

People who live here and tourists like to visit Croagh Patrick as a special trip they can take part in every year.

Ballinastoe to Djouce, County Wicklow

Ballinastoe Woods is a spot loved by hikers. Near Calary in County Wicklow, it has a long trail of 17.4 km. Many people call it hard but fun too! It winds around the woods and takes almost six hours to walk.

Djouce Mountain sits close to Ballinastoe Woods. Many take the 12 km loop trail from Ballinastoe to Djouce. This hike also includes White Hill and ends with an amazing view at a height of 725 meters!

Tips for Hiking in Ireland on a Budget

Save money on your hiking adventures in Ireland by purchasing Experience Ireland for discounted activities, exploring free or low-cost hikes, using public transportation, packing your own food and water, and considering camping instead of staying in accommodations.

Buy Experience Ireland

Buy Experience Ireland is a smart tip for saving money on your hike. This choice gives you the best rides, sights and historic places. You only pay one time! It can cut costs on entry fees to parks and museums too.

Making use of Buy Experience Ireland can help make your trip cheap and fun at the same time.

Explore free or low-cost hikes

If you're looking to go hiking in Ireland on a budget, there are plenty of free or inexpensive options to choose from. You can explore stunning landscapes without breaking the bank by checking out some of the affordable hiking trails available.

For example, The Great Sugar Loaf in County Wicklow offers breathtaking views and is accessible for hikers of all levels. Another option is the Glenbarrow Waterfall Loop in County Laois, where you can enjoy beautiful scenery and cascading waterfalls.

If you're near County Wicklow, don't miss the Bray to Greystones Cliff Walk, which offers picturesque coastal views. In County Mayo, Croagh Patrick is a popular choice for hikers seeking an enjoyable challenge.

Use public transportation

Public transportation is a great way to explore Ireland on a budget. You don't need a car to see the sights! There are many options for using public transit, such as buses and trains, which can take you to different hiking trails and scenic spots.

It's also an eco-friendly choice. Traveling by bus is often the best and most affordable way to get around the country without a car. So, hop on a bus, sit back, relax, and enjoy the stunning views of Ireland while leaving the driving to someone else.

Pack your own food and water

When hiking in Ireland on a budget, it is important to pack your own food and water. This is because there may be limited or no shops near the hiking trails where you can buy snacks and drinks.

Bringing your own supplies allows you to save money and have more control over your meals and dietary preferences. Before starting your hike, make sure to stock up on food and drinks in the town or city where you are staying.

By being self-sufficient with your provisions, you can enjoy a budget-friendly hiking experience while ensuring that you stay hydrated and energized throughout your adventure.

Camp instead of staying in accommodations

Camping is a great option for budget-friendly hiking in Ireland. With plenty of camping sites available throughout the country, you can easily find affordable outdoor accommodations close to your desired hiking trails.

Campgrounds often provide facilities like showers and toilets, ensuring basic comforts during your stay. By choosing to camp, you have the opportunity to fully immerse yourself in nature and connect with the scenic beauty of Ireland.

It's a nature-centric and affordable way to experience the outdoors while enjoying flexibility in choosing locations that suit your preferences. So why not pack up your tent and explore the stunning hiking trails Ireland has to offer?.

Recommended Easy Walks and Hikes with Epic Views

Benbulben Forest Walk in Sligo offers a breathtaking view of the majestic Benbulben Mountain, making it an easy yet rewarding hike for nature enthusiasts. Downpatrick Head in Mayo boasts stunning sea cliffs and historic ruins, providing hikers with both natural beauty and cultural significance.

Keshcorran Caves in Sligo offer a unique hiking experience through limestone caves and ancient tombs, perfect for those seeking adventure off the beaten path. The Slieve League Viewing Platform in Donegal provides awe-inspiring panoramic views of the highest sea cliffs in Europe, a must-visit for any hiking enthusiast.

Lastly, Diamond Hill - Lower Diamond Trail in Connemara, Galway takes hikers on a picturesque journey through lush valleys and beautiful woodlands before reaching the summit with sweeping views of the surrounding landscape.

Benbulben Forest Walk, Sligo

Located in Sligo, Ireland, Benbulben Forest Walk offers breathtaking views of Benbulben, known as Ireland's Table Mountain. This scenic hike is a relatively new attraction with an expanded carpark to accommodate visitors.

The trail is well-maintained and features signposted loops that can be completed in a couple of hours. It's a pleasant and mostly easy walk, making it suitable for families and friends looking to enjoy nature and the stunning vistas offered by Benbulben.

Downpatrick Head, Mayo

Downpatrick Head in County Mayo is a top destination for scenic hikes in Ireland. This stunning cliff walk offers unrivaled views of the Staggs and the Atlantic Ocean, showcasing the natural beauty of Irish landscapes.

The Downpatrick Head Loop Walk is a popular and easy route that takes around 22 minutes to complete, covering approximately 1.6km. With its breathtaking coastal views and budget-friendly appeal, Downpatrick Head is a must-visit spot for those looking to experience Ireland's wild beauty without breaking the bank.

Keshcorran Caves, Sligo

The Keshcorran Caves in Sligo offer scenic hikes and stunning views of County Sligo landscapes. Located near Keash Hill, these caves can be seen from many parts of the area. While the hike to the caves is short but steep, it's worth it for the epic panoramas you'll encounter along the way.

Keshcorran Mountain, with its un-opened cairn called the pinnacle, provides impressive viewpoints that showcase the unspoiled nature of this region. So if you're looking for a walking route with beautiful vistas and hiking opportunities, don't miss out on exploring the Keshcorran Caves in Sligo.

Slieve League Viewing Platform, Donegal

The Slieve League Viewing Platform in Donegal is a must-visit for breathtaking views of the cliffs. The trail leading up to the cliffs is 2.8 kilometers long and offers a spectacular experience.

Many hikers choose to take the Pilgrim's Path from Teelin as their preferred route to reach the cliffs. When you arrive, you'll find a visitor center and car park available for your convenience.

Comparable to the famous Cliffs of Moher, Slieve League provides an equally stunning experience at a more affordable cost for those looking for scenic hikes in Ireland.

Diamond Hill - Lower Diamond Trail, Connemara, Galway

Diamond Hill is a popular and highly recommended scenic hike located in Connemara, County Galway in Ireland. It is known for its stunning views and is suitable for all levels of hikers.

The trail is about 7 kilometers long and takes visitors through the picturesque Connemara National Park. The upper circuit of Diamond Hill offers magnificent views that are truly worth the effort.

Whether you're looking for a short walk or a rewarding hike, Diamond Hill - Lower Diamond Trail in Connemara won't disappoint with its beauty and accessibility.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Ireland offers a plethora of budget-friendly scenic hikes that cater to all abilities. From the beautiful landscapes of County Wicklow to the rugged mountains in Mayo, there is something for everyone.

So grab your backpack and explore the stunning trails and epic views that Ireland has to offer without breaking the bank. Happy hiking!

FAQs

1. Are there any budget-friendly scenic hikes in Ireland?

Yes, there are many budget-friendly scenic hikes in Ireland that offer stunning views of the countryside and coastline.

2. Do I need to pay for hiking trails in Ireland?

Most hiking trails in Ireland are free to access and do not require any payment.

3. What should I bring with me on a budget-friendly scenic hike?

It is recommended to bring comfortable walking shoes, weather-appropriate clothing, water, snacks, a map or guidebook, and a fully charged mobile phone for emergencies.

4. Can I camp overnight during a budget-friendly scenic hike?

Camping may be allowed at some designated camping areas along certain hiking routes but it's important to check local regulations beforehand.

5. Are public transportation options available near these budget-friendly scenic hikes?

Yes, many of the popular hiking locations have public transportation options nearby or can be easily accessed by bus or train from major towns and cities in Ireland.
