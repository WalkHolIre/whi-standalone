The Magic of the North: A Comprehensive Guide to the Causeway Coastal Path

Louise O'Reilly
October 12, 2023 • schedule 8 min read
The dramatic cliffs of the Causeway Coast at sunrise. Photo: Walking Holiday Ireland

Ireland’s northern coastline is a place where legends come to life. Stretching from Ballycastle to Portstewart, the Causeway Coastal Path offers some of the most spectacular walking terrain in Europe.

The Causeway Coast isn't just a walk; it's a journey through geological time and Celtic mythology. As you traverse the rugged cliff-tops, you'll encounter the famous Giant’s Causeway, where 40,000 hexagonal basalt columns descend into the Atlantic Ocean—a result of ancient volcanic activity, or as some prefer, the work of the giant Finn McCool.

When to Walk the Path

While Ireland is known for its "four seasons in one day," the best time to experience the North Coast is between late April and September. During these months, the gorse is in bloom, bathing the cliffs in a vibrant yellow hue, and the daylight lingers long into the evening.

Traditional walking paths near Dunluce Castle

Highlights Not to Miss

The 33-mile path is dotted with landmarks that require a pause. The ruins of Dunluce Castle cling precariously to the edge of a basalt outcrop, telling tales of the MacDonnell clan. Further east, the Carrick-a-Rede Rope Bridge challenges walkers to cross a 30-meter-deep chasm to reach a tiny island used by salmon fishermen for centuries.

For fans of cinematography, many locations along this route served as backdrops for Game of Thrones, including the harbor of Ballintoy, which stood in for the Iron Islands.

lightbulb Pro Tip for Hikers

Wear sturdy, waterproof hiking boots. The terrain can be slippery near the cliff edges, and the coastal weather is unpredictable. Always carry a physical map even if using GPS, as signal can be spotty in the more remote coves.
