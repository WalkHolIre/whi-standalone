## Daily Itinerary

**Day 1: Arrival in Ballycastle**
Arrive in the friendly coastal town of Ballycastle, your base for the next 4 nights. Get settled, meet your guide, and take time to acclimatise. The town has good restaurants and a genuine community feel. An evening stroll to orient yourself is recommended.

**Day 2: Ballycastle to Ballintoy**
*14 km | 4 hours*
Your first day sets the tone. Walk out of Ballycastle across moorland and rising coastal paths. The views build throughout the day — each section more dramatic than the last. Your destination is Ballintoy, a picturesque whitewashed village that appears almost painted onto the cliff face. The day culminates at the legendary Carrick-a-rede Rope Bridge: 80 feet up, suspended above a gorge, with the Atlantic churning below. Cross at your own pace, soak in the adrenaline rush, and understand why this bridge has captivated walkers for centuries. Historically, fishermen built it to access salmon-rich waters; now it's an icon.

**Day 3: Ballintoy to Bushmills**
*18 km | 5 hours | Contains tidal sections*
This is the walk that earned its reputation as "the best coastal walk in Ireland." You'll leave Ballintoy and walk into legend. The landscape unfolds in stages: White Park Bay appears — a crescent of untouched white sand beneath towering cliffs. Continue along dramatic cliff paths, passing Portbraddan (the smallest church in Ireland) and the moody, atmospheric ruins of Dunseverick Castle, perched on a sea stack. Tidal sections require timing, but they're part of the adventure — you walk where the landscape dictates, reading the coastline like a local.

Then you arrive at the Giant's Causeway: 40,000 perfectly hexagonal basalt columns, each one formed 60 million years ago during volcanic eruptions. UNESCO recognises it as a World Heritage Site; geologists marvel at it; tourists flock to it. But when you walk among the columns themselves, disconnected from the crowds at the visitor centre, something changes. You understand why this place matters. Finish in Bushmills, home to the world's oldest licensed whiskey distillery (tours optional if you're interested in learning how they make it).

**Day 4: Portballintrae to Portstewart**
*18 km | 5 hours*
Your final day pulls together everything. Walk past Dunluce Castle — those impossible ruins clinging to a sea cliff — and continue via Ramore Head, with expansive views across to Portrush and beyond. Finish in Portstewart, a vibrant seaside town with excellent restaurants and a genuine seaside atmosphere. This final walk is a celebration: you've tackled the Causeway Coast, you've earned this finish line, and the town welcomes you with the buzz of completing something genuinely meaningful.

**Day 5: Departure from Portstewart**
Head home transformed. Sore legs, incredible memories, and almost certainly already researching your next walking adventure.

## Best Time to Walk

**May to September** is prime season for the Causeway Coast. May and June bring long daylight, wildflowers on the cliffs, and relatively stable weather patterns. July and August are busier (particularly at the Giant's Causeway visitor centre), but the weather is most reliable for coastal walking. September offers fewer crowds, still-warm sea air, and beautiful autumn light that makes the cliffs glow.

**March to April** and **October** are possible but variable — expect more rainfall and shorter daylight hours. **November to February** is not recommended due to weather severity, tidal unpredictability, and limited daylight for 18 km days.

## Beste Wanderzeit

**Mai bis September** ist Hauptsaison für die Causeway Coast. Mai und Juni bringen lange Tageslichtstunden, Wildblumen auf den Klippen und relativ stabile Wettermuster. Juli und August sind überlaufener (besonders beim Giant's Causeway Besucherzentrum), aber das Wetter ist zuverlässigster für Küstenwanderungen. September bietet weniger Menschenmengen, noch warme Meeresluft und schönes Herbstlicht, das die Klippen zum Glühen bringt.

**März bis April** und **Oktober** sind möglich, aber variabel — erwarten Sie mehr Niederschlag und kürzere Tageslichtstunden. **November bis Februar** wird nicht empfohlen aufgrund von Wetterernsthaftigkeit, Gezeitenunvorhersehbarkeit und begrenztem Tageslicht für 18-km-Tage.
