Sheep graze on impossible slopes. Every 2km reveals a new vista. You finish in Ballyvaughan, a picturesque fishing village on Galway Bay, understanding that you've walked through geology, history, and natural beauty that rewrote your understanding of Ireland.

## Best Time to Walk

**May to September** is when the Burren is at its absolute best. May and June are peak wildflower season—the limestone pavement erupts with Arctic, Alpine, and Mediterranean plants in bloom. Days are long, the light is golden, and the weather is (usually) cooperative. Temperatures range 15–18°C.

September brings clearer skies, fewer crowds, and that particular golden light of autumn. The Burren in autumn is serene and beautiful. Summer (July–August) is warmer but busier with tourists, though the Burren absorbs crowds better than most Irish landscapes.

Avoid winter (November–February) unless you're specifically seeking solitude and don't mind cold, wet, short daylight. Spring (March–April) can still be rainy, though wildflowers are beginning to emerge.
