# 10 Best European Hiking Tours: Why Ireland Leads in 2026

Long-distance hiking in Europe has exploded in popularity, with pilgrims and adventurers choosing from hundreds of trails across the continent. This guide reviews the 10 best European hiking experiences and explains why Ireland deserves top consideration for your next adventure.

## A Brief History of European Hiking Trails

Long-distance hiking as we know it originated in Germany over a century ago, with the first waymarked trails created to provide recreational walking access. Since then, hundreds of trails have been established across Europe, with pilgrim routes (particularly the Camino de Santiago) reviving ancient pathways and introducing millions to multi-day hiking.

Today, Europe offers unmatched hiking diversity—from coastal paths to alpine peaks, cultural routes to wilderness adventures.

## The 10 Best European Hiking Tours

### #1: The Camino de Santiago (Spain & Portugal)

**Distance**: 780+ km (varies by route)
**Duration**: 30-40 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2 (Moderate)
**Why it's iconic**: Ancient pilgrim route with spiritual significance. Mix of hiking, culture, history, and personal transformation.

**Highlights:**
- Well-marked, well-established trail system
- Abundant accommodations and support services
- Spiritual/reflective experience
- Diverse landscapes (Pyrenees, plateaus, coasts)
- Active hiking community with built-in social aspects

**Drawbacks:**
- Very crowded (especially June-August)
- Can feel commercialized
- Accommodation quality varies widely
- Limited luggage transfer options

**Best season**: April-May, September-October

### #2: The Wicklow Way (Ireland)

**Distance**: 131 km
**Duration**: 7-8 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2 (Moderate)
**Why it's exceptional**: Ireland's original waymarked trail. Perfect introduction to serious hiking with excellent accommodation and cultural immersion.

**Highlights:**
- Ireland's first long-distance trail (established 1980)
- Glendalough monastic site (one of Europe's greatest historical treasures)
- Mountain scenery close to Dublin
- Excellent accommodation network
- [INTERNAL LINK: Luggage transfer services → grading-of-hiking-tours] included
- Less crowded than other major European trails

**Drawbacks:**
- Shorter than epic European routes
- Weather changeable (but manageable)
- Must train 8-12 weeks for best experience

**Best season**: May-September

### #3: Mont Blanc Circuit (France, Italy, Switzerland)

**Distance**: 170 km
**Duration**: 10-11 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2-3 (Moderate to Challenging)
**Why it's dramatic**: Circumnavigates Europe's highest peak with alpine scenery, glacier views, and multiple countries in one trek.

**Highlights:**
- Alpine scenery unmatched in Europe
- Highest peak on the continent
- Multiple countries/cultures in one trek
- Incredible panoramic views
- Well-marked, established trail

**Drawbacks:**
- Altitude challenging for some (passes 2,600m)
- Early/late season snow possible
- Significant elevation gain (1,000m+ daily)
- High cost (alpine huts expensive)
- Requires solid fitness

**Best season**: July-September

### #4: The Dolomites (Italy)

**Distance**: Varies (100-300 km)
**Duration**: 5-15 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2-3 (Moderate to Challenging)
**Why it's special**: Dramatic limestone peaks, scenic valleys, mountain hut culture, excellent food and wine.

**Highlights:**
- Otherworldly rock formations
- Famous mountain hut network (rifugios)
- Italian hospitality and cuisine
- Cable car alternatives available
- Mix of challenging peaks and easier valleys

**Drawbacks:**
- Weather unpredictable (afternoon thunderstorms)
- More difficult navigation than other trails
- Alpine huts can be crowded
- Higher altitude means altitude sickness possible
- Significant climbing required

**Best season**: July-September

### #5: The Kungsleden (Sweden)

**Distance**: 440 km
**Duration**: 30 days (or do sections)
**Difficulty**: Grade 2-3 (Moderate to Challenging)
**Why it's unique**: Wilderness hiking above the Arctic Circle. Midnight sun, pristine nature, minimal crowds, genuine remoteness.

**Highlights:**
- True wilderness experience
- Midnight sun (midnight light)
- Mountain hut network
- Pristine water for drinking
- Reindeer herds, Arctic wildlife
- Solitude and nature immersion

**Drawbacks:**
- Extremely remote (isolation intense)
- Weather harsh even in summer
- Short hiking season (June-September only)
- Expensive mountain hut accommodations
- Requires bushcraft skills
- Isolation can feel overwhelming

**Best season**: July-August only

### #6: The West Highland Way (Scotland)

**Distance**: 96 km
**Duration**: 5-7 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2 (Moderate)
**Why it's beautiful**: Scottish Highlands, lochs, glens, Ben Nevis views, genuine Highland culture.

**Highlights:**
- Scottish Highlands scenery
- Well-maintained trail
- Excellent accommodation
- Ben Nevis views (Britain's highest peak)
- Strong Highland culture
- Established hiking community

**Drawbacks:**
- Can be muddy (Scottish weather)
- Midges (biting insects) mid-summer
- Popular = crowded in peak season
- Weather unpredictable
- Short trail (only 96 km)

**Best season**: May-June, September-October

### #7: The Coast-to-Coast Walk (England)

**Distance**: 192 km
**Duration**: 10-12 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2 (Moderate)
**Why it's appealing**: Traverse England from west coast to east, diverse landscapes, excellent infrastructure, Pennine Mountains.

**Highlights:**
- Diverse English landscape
- Pennine Mountains
- Established accommodations
- Cultural diversity (villages, towns)
- Achievable challenge for most hikers
- Popular hiking community

**Drawbacks:**
- Rolling terrain (constant ups and downs)
- Weather changeable
- Very popular (crowded)
- Some road walking
- UK prices (expensive)

**Best season**: May-September

### #8: The Dalmatian Coast (Croatia)

**Distance**: 130+ km (various routes)
**Duration**: 7-10 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 1-2 (Easy to Moderate)
**Why it's scenic**: Coastal trails, island hopping, Mediterranean scenery, affordable European destination, emerging trail infrastructure.

**Highlights:**
- Adriatic Sea coastal scenery
- Island-hopping opportunities
- Mediterranean culture and food
- Affordable compared to Western Europe
- Warm, sunny weather (summer)
- Growing trail infrastructure

**Drawbacks:**
- Trail infrastructure less developed than Western Europe
- Summer heat extreme
- Growing crowds as popularity increases
- Some navigation challenges
- Limited water sources in summer

**Best season**: May-June, September-October (avoid August heat)

### #9: The Cinque Terre (Italy)

**Distance**: 12 km (trail section only)
**Duration**: 1-2 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 1 (Easy)
**Why it's special**: UNESCO World Heritage coastal villages, terraced vineyards, Mediterranean charm, historic villages connected by trail.

**Highlights:**
- UNESCO World Heritage sites
- Dramatic coastal scenery
- Cultural village immersion
- Short but intense experience
- Mediterranean food and wine
- Italian Riviera beauty

**Drawbacks:**
- Extremely crowded (millions annual visitors)
- Trail damaged/partially closed some periods
- Minimal wilderness experience
- Expensive accommodations
- Can feel touristy
- Very short (not a full hiking holiday)

**Best season**: April-May, September-October

### #10: The Kerry Way (Ireland)

**Distance**: 215 km
**Duration**: 9-10 days
**Difficulty**: Grade 2-3 (Moderate to Challenging)
**Why it's exceptional**: Ireland's longest trail, mountain and coastal scenery, includes Carrauntoohil (Ireland's highest peak), cultural immersion.

**Highlights:**
- Longest signposted Irish trail
- Carrauntoohil (1,038m highest peak)
- Macgillycuddy's Reeks mountains
- Killarney National Park
- Circular route (scenic from all angles)
- [INTERNAL LINK: Luggage transfer included → grading-of-hiking-tours]
- Less crowded than international famous trails

**Drawbacks:**
- More challenging than other Irish trails
- Requires serious training (12+ weeks)
- Mountain weather unpredictable
- Requires good fitness level

**Best season**: June-September
