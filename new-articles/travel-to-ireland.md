<h2>Getting to Ireland: Transportation Options</h2>

<h3>Flying to Ireland</h3>

<p><strong>Major Irish airports:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Dublin Airport (DUB):</strong> Ireland's largest, best connections internationally, 70 km south of [INTERNAL LINK: Blackrock base → blackrock-village-county-louth]</li>
<li><strong>Cork Airport (ORK):</strong> Southern Ireland, good for southwest region exploration</li>
<li><strong>Shannon Airport (SNN):</strong> West coast access, serves Galway/Connemara region</li>
<li><strong>Belfast International (BFS):</strong> Northern Ireland access (requires UK ETA/visa—[INTERNAL LINK: see UK ETA requirements → northern-ireland-uk-eta-guide])</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Airlines serving Ireland:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Aer Lingus:</strong> Ireland's national carrier, extensive US/EU connections</li>
<li><strong>Ryanair:</strong> European budget carrier, numerous international routes</li>
<li><strong>TUI Airways:</strong> UK-based carrier with good European connections</li>
<li><strong>Major US carriers:</strong> United, Delta, American Airlines (seasonal)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Flight booking tips:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li>Book 2-3 months in advance for best prices</li>
<li>Transatlantic flights typically depart US in evening, arrive Dublin morning (next day local time)</li>
<li>Return flights depart Ireland evening, arrive US same evening (due to time zones)</li>
<li>Baggage allowance varies—confirm before booking</li>
</ul>

<h3>Ferry to Ireland</h3>

<p><strong>Ferry operators and routes:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Irish Ferries:</strong> Routes from France (Cherbourg, Roscoff) to Cork/Rosslare</li>
<li><strong>Stenaline:</strong> UK routes to Dublin, Dun Laoghaire, Belfast</li>
<li><strong>P&O Ferries:</strong> UK routes to Dublin, Larne (Northern Ireland)</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Ferry details:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li>Travel time: 8-17 hours depending on route</li>
<li>Vehicles accommodated on most routes</li>
<li>Cabin accommodation available on longer crossings</li>
<li>Book in advance, especially peak season</li>
</ul>

<h3>Getting Around Ireland</h3>

<p><strong>Car rental:</strong> Most convenient for self-guided walking holidays (luggage transport, trail accessibility)</p>

<ul>
<li>Drive on the left (British-style)</li>
<li>Roads generally good, rural roads narrow</li>
<li>Petrol/diesel readily available</li>
<li>Full driving license and International Permit recommended</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>Public transportation:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li><strong>Irish Rail:</strong> www.irishrail.ie—connects major cities</li>
<li><strong>Bus services:</strong> Bus Éireann, GoBus connect regional areas</li>
<li><strong>Dublin transport:</strong> DART (commuter rail), Luas (tram), extensive bus network</li>
<li><strong>Journey Planner:</strong> http://www.journeyplanner.transportforireland.ie for route planning</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>For Northern Ireland:</strong> Translink (www.translink.co.uk) provides bus and rail services</p>

<p><strong>Taxis/ride-share:</strong> Available in cities, more limited in rural areas</p>

<h2>Travel Documentation Requirements</h2>

<h3>Passports</h3>

<p><strong>Essential for international travel:</strong></p>

<ul>
<li>Must be valid for entire stay (6+ months recommended beyond return date)</li>
<li>Standard validity: 10 years (adults), 5 years (children)</li>
<li>Passport renewal: Begin 6+ months before expiration if international travel planned</li>
<li>Check passport well before booking—processing can take weeks</li>
</ul>

<p><strong>UK citizens:</strong> Photo ID alternatives accepted within Common Travel Area</p>

<h3>Visas & Travel Authorization</h3>

<p><strong>Republic of Ireland:</strong> Visa requirements vary by nationality</p>

<p><strong>No visa required for:</strong> EEA nations, USA, Canada, Australia, New Zealand, and many others (visit www.inis.gov.ie for complete list)</p>

<p><strong>Visa required for:</strong> Some nationalities—check with Irish embassy/consulate</p>

<p><strong>If planning to drive:</strong></p>

<ul>

<h2>Practical Tips for First-Time Irish Travelers</h2>

<ul>
<li><strong>Left-side driving:</strong> Takes adjustment—allow extra time until comfortable</li>
<li><strong>Weather preparation:</strong> Pack waterproof jacket and layers regardless of season</li>
<li><strong>Pubs:</strong> Social centers, not just drinking establishments—visit for culture and connection</li>
<li><strong>Distances:</strong> Irish roads are slower than appearing—budget extra travel time</li>
<li><strong>Accommodation booking:</strong> Book in advance, especially peak season and weekends</li>
<li><strong>Walking culture:</strong> Hikers are respected; ask permission before crossing private property</li>
</ul>

<h2>Frequently Asked Questions</h2>

<h3>Do I need a visa to travel to Ireland?</h3>

<p>Depends on your nationality. Citizens of EEA, USA, Canada, Australia, and New Zealand typically don't require visas. Check www.inis.gov.ie for your specific nationality.</p>

<h3>What's the best way to get around Ireland?</h3>

<p>For walking holidays, car rental provides maximum flexibility. Public transport is available but more limited in rural areas where hiking trails are located.</p>

<h3>Can I drive my US/UK car in Ireland?</h3>

<p>Yes, with valid license, registration, and international insurance coverage (Green Card). Vehicles must have GB/UK identifier and headlights adjusted for left-side driving.</p>

<h3>What's the time difference?</h3>

<p>Ireland is on GMT/UTC (winter) or IST (Irish Standard Time, summer). Five hours ahead of US Eastern Time.</p>

<h3>Is tap water safe to drink?</h3>

<p>Yes, Irish tap water is among Europe's safest and highest quality. No need for bottled water unless preferred.</p>
