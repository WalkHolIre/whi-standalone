# Rare Flora & Fauna in Ireland: Wildlife Guide for Hikers

Ireland's landscapes host distinctive wildlife species that reward observant hikers with memorable encounters. Learn what you might discover while hiking Irish trails.

## Remarkable Land Animals

### Native Irish Red Deer
**Where**: Killarney National Park (Kerry Way), Connemara
**Characteristics**: Graceful, regal bearing; larger than European cousins
**Significance**: Ireland's only surviving native red deer herd
**Hiking**: Best viewed early morning or dusk from Kerry Way

### White-Tailed Eagles
**Where**: Northwestern Ireland
**Characteristics**: Massive, powerful raptors; wedge-shaped white tail
**Significance**: Once extinct, now reintroduced—conservation success story
**Hiking**: Rare sightings; look for large silhouettes near water

### Natterjack Toad
**Where**: The Burren region (County Clare)
**Characteristics**: Yellow stripe down back; loud nocturnal calls
**Significance**: One of Ireland's rarest animals; endangered
**Hiking**: Heard more than seen; listen for distinctive trill at dusk

## Remarkable Marine Life

### Seabirds
**Puffins** (60% of world population breeds in Ireland!)
- **Season**: April-August
- **Location**: Skellig Islands, Cliffs of Moher, Rathlin Island
- **Characteristics**: Distinctive orange beak, charming appearance

**Gannets, Razorbills, Guillemots, Kittiwakes**
- **Location**: Coastal cliffs throughout Ireland
- **Season**: April-September breeding
- **Viewing**: Coastal hiking, boat trips

### Seals
**Grey & Common Seals**
- **Location**: Rocky shores, sandy beaches
- **Characteristics**: Distinct habitat preferences
- **Viewing**: Early morning from coastal trails
- **Ethics**: Maintain 50+ meter distance

## Remarkable Flora

### Wild Orchids
**Location**: The Burren, Dingle Peninsula
**Season**: Late spring-summer
**Characteristics**: Diverse species, delicate beauty
**Protection**: Don't pick; photograph instead
**[INTERNAL LINK: Environmental responsibility → guidelines-for-minimising-environmental-impact-while-hiking-in-ireland]**

### Rare Wildflowers
**Sea Kale, Buttercups, Primrose, Foxglove**
- **Location**: Varies by trail and season
- **Hiking**: Most abundant May-July
- **Photography**: Capture without harm
- **Significance**: Many species endangered by over-collection

### Ancient Oak Forests
**Location**: Wicklow Way, Kerry Way
**Characteristics**: Some trees 500+ years old
**Ecological**: Home to rare insects and bird species
**Protection**: Never damage; stay on trails

## Best Hikes for Wildlife Viewing

### For Seabirds
**Coastal Trails**: Causeway Coast, Dingle Way, Cooley Peninsula
**Best Season**: April-August
**Tip**: Early morning for peak activity

### For Mountain Wildlife
**Kerry Way**: Red deer, eagles possible
**Wicklow Way**: Various woodland creatures
**Best Time**: Dawn/dusk

### For Rare Flora
**The Burren (3-4 day trails)**: Orchids, wildflowers
**Dingle Way**: Coastal plants, mountain flora
**Season**: Late May-July

### For Seals & Coastal Animals
**Cooley Peninsula**: Easy coastal access
**Causeway Coast**: Dramatic clifftop viewing
**Coastal sections of any trail**: Early morning best

## Wildlife Photography Tips

**Ethical Practices:**
- Maintain distance (50+ meters for most wildlife)
- Never pursue animals
- Use telephoto lens instead of approaching
- Don't disrupt natural behavior
- Never photograph people without permission

**Camera Settings:**
- Fast shutter speed for moving creatures
- Zoom lens 300mm+ for distant subjects
- Quiet mode to minimize disturbance
- Tripod for steady support

**Best Time**: Early morning (6-8 AM), dusk (7-9 PM)

## FAQ: Irish Wildlife

**When is best for birdwatching?**
April-August breeding season. May-June peak activity.

**Where do I see puffins?**
Skellig Islands (boat trip), Cliffs of Moher, Rathlin Island (accessible).

**Are seals dangerous?**
No. Maintain distance; they're shy. Never approach.

**Can I pick wildflowers?**
No. Photograph instead. Rare species face extinction.

**What if I see injured wildlife?**
Contact local authorities or wildlife rescue centers.
