Walk Ireland freely. We handle logistics—B&Bs, luggage, maps, GPS, and 24/7 support. 15 routes from €610.

What Are Self-Guided Walking Tours?

Self-guided walking tours give you the freedom to explore Ireland's most stunning landscapes at your own pace, with none of the logistical stress. You walk each stage alone or with friends, stopping to rest, eat, and admire the views whenever you like. No group schedule. No keeping up. No compromises.

What makes this different from just "walking around Ireland" is the support system. We book your accommodation in advance—comfortable, handpicked B&Bs in family homes where you'll meet other walkers. We transfer your luggage daily so you walk with just a day pack. We provide GPS navigation, detailed walking notes, and local expertise. And if anything goes wrong, we're a phone call away.

Ireland is perfect for self-guided walking. The trails are well-waymarked, the landscape is endlessly varied, and the people are genuinely warm. Whether you're a solo traveler seeking independence, a couple wanting flexibility, or a small group wanting to move at your own speed, this is the way to experience Ireland on foot.

Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need to be very fit to do a self-guided walking tour? add

No. Our tours span a range of difficulty levels. We offer easy walks (Barrow Way, some Burren stages) perfect for beginners, moderate walks for those with some experience, and challenging routes for experienced walkers. You walk at your own pace—if a stage is too long, you can cover it in two days. See our Tour Grading & Difficulty page for full details on each route.

What if I get lost on the trail? add

You won't. Every tour includes our GPS app with accurate offline routing, detailed walking notes with landmarks and directions, and official waymarked trails. You also have 24/7 local support by phone if you ever need guidance—just call and we'll help.

Can I customize my itinerary? add

Yes. You can extend or shorten your tour, skip certain stages, reverse the direction, or adjust your walking pace. We also offer fully tailor-made self-guided tours designed around your specific interests and fitness level.

What happens to my luggage while I walk? add

We transfer your luggage daily between B&Bs. You leave your bag at breakfast, and it's waiting for you at your next accommodation in the afternoon. You just walk with a day pack (5-8kg) containing snacks, a rain jacket, and water. Simple and stress-free.

When is the best time to walk in Ireland? add

May to September offers the warmest, most stable weather and longest daylight. April and October are beautiful with fewer crowds and brilliant light. Winter (November–February) is possible but expect shorter daylight, cooler temperatures, and changeable conditions. Peak season (July–August) is busy but rewarding—book early.

Can I do a self-guided walking tour solo? add

Absolutely. Many of our walkers travel solo and love the freedom and independence. You'll meet other travelers at your B&Bs in the evenings (communal breakfasts, pub dinners), and you have 24/7 support from us if you need advice, weather updates, or just someone to talk to.
